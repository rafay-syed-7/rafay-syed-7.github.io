About me

Hey all! My name is Rafay Syed, a current computer science student at Williams College. In my free time I like to read, play squash, watch movies, and explore the outdoors.

Over the course of this semester I have taken a HCI class here at Williams. Throughout this course I learned about the design process, from the ideation process all the way to the actual designing of interfaces.

Throughout the course of this class I have completed six design sprints that has helped me refine my design philosophy. I've learned about how to design for interactivity, accessibility, visual appeal, and more. Feel free to take a look at my design sprint portfolio. Thanks for browsing!

DS1: Needfinding

I explored WHOOP to understand how users interact with the technology, the needs it fulfills or overlooks, and the insights revealed through their daily experiences.

DS2: Designing for Others

I redesigned the Williams Study Away website to a mobile interface catered towards International Students.

DS3: Designing for Interactivity

We remapped traditional inputs to gesture-based controls for the popular game 2048.

DS4: Designing for Effectiveness

We redesigned an existing book catalog website to improve its usability and visual clarity, then evaluated our changes through user testing to assess the effectiveness of our design decisions

DS5: Accessibility Audits

I evaluated Amazons interface through the lens of users with visual disabilities looking at accessibility standards and first-hand accounts. I identified barriers and proposed design improvements.

DS6: Design in Practice

My team created Pulse, a browser extension that acts as a productivity timer to promote focused work sessions. Subtle nudges help reduce online distraction and encourage intentionality while online.
